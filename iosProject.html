Quick Maths

An iOS Arithmetic Game

Project Background

The rise of the mobile application scene came to be in the year 2008 when Apple launched their App Store, which created a centralised marketplace for third-party applications. Nowadays, it has become a common tool for learning and practicing academic skills. The educational apps allow users to engage with learning materials in an interactive and convenient way. Traditional pen and paper methods for arithmetic practice are slowly getting replaced by digital solutions that make learning more engaging.

Quick Maths is a simple iOS game that will help users practice their mental arithmetic skills, which is beneficial for mathematics as repetitive practice can help with calculation speed and accuracy. The application will provide randomly generated arithmetic problems that users solve in a game-like environment.

While seemingly simple, Quick Maths incorporate different difficulty levels to keep the users engaged and challenged. This approach allows the users to progressively enhance their arithmetic abilities. Blending learning with gameplay makes mathematics practices fun and enjoyable.

Features

  • Difficulty Levels
    • Easy - basic addition and subtraction problems with numbers under 100
    • Medium - includes multiplication and is not limited to two numbers to compute
    • Hard - more complex and introducing PEMDAS
  • Random Arithmetic Equations
    • The game will generate random numbers, arithmetic operators, and the location of the operands. This ensures that the game can be played repeatedly and reduces the chance of seeing the same equation.
  • Score + Lives System
    • The game tracks the score based on the correct answers made by the user. Additionally, it punishes incorrect answers by deducting the user's life. Three incorrect answers and the game ends, which encourages both speed and accuracy.
  • Simple User Interface
    • The interface will be designed to be minimal, and easy to navigate.
  • Leaderboards
    • Aside from being a simple practice tool, the game will recognise those who aim to improve and prove their skill by beating the score of other people.

Scope and Limitations

The scope of this project is intentionally limited to ensure that it can be completed within the allocated development timeframe. The following is the scope of this project:

  • User login/Sign up
    • Email and password authentication using Firebase
    • No third-party authentication such as Google or Facebook
  • Difficulty: Easy
    • Uses only addition and subtraction
    • Limited to two numbers less than 100
    • Randomised number and operator
  • Difficulty: Medium
    • Includes multiplication in the equation
    • Can reach up to four (4) numbers in the equation
    • Less than 100 for addition and subtraction, Less than 11 for multiplication
  • Difficulty: Hard
    • Introduces parenthesis in the equation
    • Can reach up to four (4) numbers in the equation
    • Less than 100 for addition and subtraction, Less than 11 for multiplication
  • Leaderboard
    • Grabs data from Firebase to display the top 10 players
  • Game Mechanics
    • Users must type the answer
    • Score system gives 1 point for every correct answer
    • Lives system ends the game after three incorrect answers
    • Each game session lasts 60 seconds, players must answer as many equations as possible before the time runs out.

Challenges & Lessons Learned

The biggest challenge in developing the iOS app is working with the constraints. Compared to my experience with Android development, I found it less intuitive and more time consuming to implement. The view layouts were ones that I have not found any success in playing around with due to time constraints. Currently, the app has limited support for landscape orientation.

On the other hand, my prior programming experience helped me quickly adapt to the development process. Once I became familiar with XCode, storyboards, and segues, I was able to work much faster. Overall, this project was a valuable learning experience where I gained a much better understanding of iOS development environment as well as the Swift language.
